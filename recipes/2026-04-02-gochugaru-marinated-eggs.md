# Gochugaru-Marinated Eggs (Mayak Gyeran — Korean "Drug" Eggs)

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Serves:** 4 eggs
**Prep time:** 10 min | **Cook time:** 7 min | **Marinate:** 6–12 hrs | **Total:** ~12 hrs
**Status:** Final

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Large eggs | 4 | Straight from fridge |
| Soy sauce | 80ml (80g) | |
| Water | 60ml (60g) | |
| Rice vinegar | 15ml (15g) | Brightness to cut the heat |
| Sugar | 15g | |
| Gochugaru | 8g (~1 Tbsp) | Korean red pepper flakes — fruity, moderate heat |
| Garlic | 2 cloves (10g) | Minced |
| Scallions | 2 stalks (20g) | Sliced into rings |
| Sesame seeds | 5g | Toasted |

## Method

1. **Boil eggs 6 min 30 sec** in rolling boiling water. Ice bath 5 min. Peel.
2. **Make marinade.** Stir soy, water, rice vinegar, and sugar until sugar dissolves (no heat needed). Add gochugaru, garlic, scallions, and sesame seeds.
3. **Marinate.** Submerge eggs in a jar or zip-lock. Refrigerate **6–12 hrs**, flipping once. The gochugaru particles cling to the whites, staining them a brick-orange and depositing capsaicin for slow-build heat.
4. **Serve.** Halve over hot rice. Spoon the scallion-gochugaru bits on top.

## Quick Rationale

Mayak gyeran ("drug eggs") are addictive because they hit every taste receptor — salty, sweet, spicy, umami, and acidic. The gochugaru provides gentle heat without the vinegar punch of gochujang, letting the egg flavor come through.

## Notes & Adjustments

- Add 5ml sesame oil to the marinade for nuttiness
- These keep 3 days refrigerated; flavor intensifies daily

# Shoyu Ajitsuke Tamago (Soy-Marinated Ramen Eggs)

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Serves:** 4 eggs
**Prep time:** 10 min | **Cook time:** 7 min | **Marinate:** 4–12 hrs | **Total:** ~12 hrs
**Status:** Final

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Large eggs | 4 | Straight from fridge |
| Soy sauce | 80ml (80g) | Regular, not low-sodium |
| Mirin | 60ml (66g) | Hon-mirin preferred |
| Water | 60ml (60g) | To temper salinity |
| Sugar | 10g | Dissolves in warm marinade |
| Garlic | 1 clove (5g) | Lightly crushed |

## Method

1. **Boil eggs.** Bring 1.5L water to a rolling boil. Lower cold eggs in gently with a spoon. Cook **6 min 30 sec** for jammy, **7 min** for fudgy. At 6:30 the yolk proteins (lipovitellin) are ~70% set — giving that orange-centered custard.
2. **Ice bath immediately.** Transfer to ice water for 5 min. This halts carryover cooking and contracts the egg away from the shell for easy peeling.
3. **Peel carefully.** Tap on the counter, roll gently, peel under running water.
4. **Make marinade.** Combine soy, mirin, water, sugar, and garlic in a small saucepan. Warm just until sugar dissolves. Cool to room temp.
5. **Marinate.** Place peeled eggs in a zip-lock bag or small container with marinade. Refrigerate **4 hrs minimum, 12 hrs ideal**. Turn halfway. Osmosis drives soy and sugar into the white — longer = deeper color and more savory.
6. **Serve.** Halve lengthwise. Eat on ramen, rice, or alone.

## Quick Rationale

Classic ramen-shop formula. The mirin adds sweetness that balances soy salinity and promotes a glossy lacquer. The short cook time keeps the yolk runny enough to enrich broth when broken.

## Notes & Adjustments

- Beyond 24 hrs the whites get rubbery and overly salty — pull them out
- For deeper color, use a 1:1 soy:mirin ratio with no water

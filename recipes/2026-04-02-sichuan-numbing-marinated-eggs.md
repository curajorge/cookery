# Sichuan Numbing Marinated Eggs (Ma La Lu Dan)

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Serves:** 4 eggs
**Prep time:** 15 min | **Cook time:** 12 min | **Marinate:** 6–24 hrs | **Total:** ~24 hrs
**Status:** Final

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Large eggs | 4 | Straight from fridge |
| Soy sauce | 60ml (60g) | Light soy (sheng chou) preferred |
| Water | 120ml (120g) | |
| Shaoxing wine | 30ml (30g) | Dry sherry works |
| Sugar | 10g | |
| Sichuan peppercorns | 5g (~1 Tbsp) | Toasted and lightly crushed |
| Dried red chilies | 4 (6g) | Facing heaven or arbol, snipped open |
| Star anise | 1 whole | |
| Garlic | 2 cloves (10g) | Smashed |
| Fresh ginger | 8g | 2 coins, smashed |
| Sesame oil | 5ml (5g) | Finishing |

## Method

1. **Boil eggs 6 min 30 sec.** Ice bath 5 min. Peel.
2. **Toast aromatics.** In a dry small saucepan over medium heat (~160C/320F), toast Sichuan peppercorns for 60 sec until fragrant and just smoking — this activates the hydroxy-alpha-sanshool compound responsible for the numbing sensation. Add dried chilies, star anise, garlic, and ginger. Toast 30 sec more.
3. **Build the brine.** Add soy sauce, water, Shaoxing wine, and sugar. Bring to a simmer for 3 min to meld flavors. Cool completely.
4. **Marinate.** Place eggs in a jar, pour cooled brine over them. Refrigerate **6 hrs minimum, 24 hrs ideal**. The numbing tingle from the peppercorns intensifies slowly — at 24 hrs it's electric.
5. **Serve.** Halve, drizzle with sesame oil. Pair with congee, noodles, or cold beer.

## Quick Rationale

Ma la (numbing-spicy) is Sichuan's signature flavor profile. Toasting the peppercorns before building the brine maximizes extraction of the volatile numbing compounds. The star anise adds a warm anise backbone that anchors the heat.

## Notes & Adjustments

- For more heat, add 5g doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste) to the simmering brine
- The brine can be reused once — reboil, cool, and add fresh eggs

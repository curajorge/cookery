# Fettuccine in Creamy Tomato Pancetta + Tiras de Asado

**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Serves:** 2
**Prep time:** 20 min | **Cook time:** 35 min | **Total:** 55 min (includes 30 min dough rest)
**Status:** Draft

## Part 1 — Fresh Fettuccine

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 00 flour (Tipo 00) | 200g | Caputo red bag |
| Whole eggs | 2 (~100g) | Room temp |
| Egg yolk | 1 (~17g) | Extra yolk = richer, more golden |
| Fine salt | 2g | Into the dough |
| Semolina or extra flour | 30g | For dusting |

1. Flour + salt in KitchenAid bowl, paddle. Eggs + yolk in, mix low until shaggy (1–2 min)
2. Switch to dough hook, knead medium-low 8–10 min — smooth, elastic, springs back
3. Wrap plastic, rest 30 min room temp. *Gluten must relax or it fights the roller*
4. Divide dough in 4. Roll through KitchenAid roller: start widest setting, work down to setting 5 (~2mm)
5. Switch to fettuccine cutter attachment, feed sheets through
6. Toss in semolina, nest loosely on tray

---

## Part 2 — Tiras de Asado (Air Fryer)

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Flanken-cut short ribs | 450g | ~1cm thick cross-cut, bone-in |
| Coarse kosher salt | 5g | Both sides, 20 min before cooking |

1. Salt ribs on both sides, rest on rack 20 min uncovered. *Draws moisture, reabsorbs, seasons deeper — Nosrat principle*
2. **Preheat Wonder Oven Pro** → Air Fry, 400°F, 5 min. Deep bake pan on bottom rack to catch drip
3. Pat ribs dry with paper towels
4. Place ribs on middle rack, single layer, no overlap
5. Cook **6–7 min per side**. Target: browned crust, slightly pink center
6. Rest 3 min on cutting board. Cut between bones to separate

*Argentine asado philosophy: salt + fire + good beef. Nothing else needed. The marbling in flanken ribs renders into the meat during air fry convection.*

## Part 3 — Creamy Tomato Pancetta Sauce

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pancetta (cubed) | 100g | Already cut — lardons |
| San Marzano tomatoes | 250g | Just under half the can, crush by hand |
| Heavy cream | 90g | |
| Garlic | 2 cloves (~10g) | Minced |
| Red chili flakes | 1g | |
| Pecorino Romano | 40g | Microplane, right before use |
| Parmigiano-Reggiano | 20g | Microplane |
| Black pepper | 2g | Fresh cracked |
| Kosher salt (pasta water) | 30g | For 3L water (1% by weight) |
| Pasta water (reserved) | 100g | Starchy liquid gold |

1. Pancetta in cold pan, medium-low, render 8–10 min until fat is glassy and edges crisp. *Cold start = even render, no burned exterior with raw center*
2. Add garlic + chili flakes to the fat, 30 sec until fragrant. *Bloom aromatics in pork fat — volatile compounds activate*
3. Hand-crush San Marzano tomatoes into the pan. Simmer 8–10 min, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens and oil separates at edges
4. Add cream, stir through, simmer 2 min. *Cream stabilizes the emulsion and rounds the tomato acidity*
5. Season with pepper. Taste — the pancetta and cheese add salt, so hold off until after cheese goes in
6. Cook fettuccine in 3L boiling water + 30g salt, **2–3 min only** (it's fresh)
7. Reserve 100g pasta water before draining
8. Using tongs, transfer the drained fettuccine directly into the pan with the tomato-cream sauce. Add 50g of the reserved pasta water. Toss over low heat for 30 sec so the pasta absorbs the sauce
9. Kill the heat. Add pecorino + parm, toss vigorously for 15 sec. If the sauce looks too thick, add more pasta water a splash at a time until it coats the noodles like silk
10. Plate and finish with a drizzle of good EVOO (~10g) — adds brightness against the cream and pork fat

## Execution Timeline

1. **Make pasta dough** → KitchenAid, 10 min. Wrap, rest 30 min
2. **Salt the ribs** → both sides on rack, set 20 min timer
3. **While dough rests** → mince garlic, crush tomatoes, grate cheeses, prep mise en place
4. **Roll + cut fettuccine** → roller then cutter attachment, dust with semolina, nest on tray
5. **Start Wonder Oven Pro** → Air Fry 400°F, preheat 5 min
6. **Air fry ribs** → 6–7 min per side. Rest when done
7. **Start pancetta** → cold pan, render 8–10 min
8. **Boil pasta water** → 3L + 30g salt (start this when pancetta goes in)
9. **Build sauce** → garlic + chili → tomatoes → cream
10. **Drop fettuccine** → 2–3 min, reserve water
11. **Finish pasta** → toss in sauce, cheese off heat
12. **Plate** → twirl fettuccine, ribs alongside, extra pecorino on top

---

## Quick Rationale

Creamy tomato pancetta is the vodka-sauce cousin — same architecture (rendered pork fat + tomato + cream + hard cheese) without the alcohol. The cream does the heavy lifting: emulsifies the sauce, rounds tomato acidity, and coats fresh fettuccine better than a lean tomato sauce would. Flanken ribs air-fried at 400°F get Maillard crust from convection heat while the intramuscular fat renders — Argentine simplicity meets countertop convenience.

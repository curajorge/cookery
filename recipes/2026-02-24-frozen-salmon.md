# Frozen Salmon — Straight From Freezer, Our Place Wonder Oven Pro

**Date:** 2026-02-24
**Serves:** 2
**Prep time:** 3 min | **Cook time:** 22 min | **Total:** 25 min
**Status:** Draft

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Frozen salmon fillets (skin-on) | 2 fillets, ~170g each | Straight from freezer, do not thaw |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 10g | Heat conductor + spice adhesive on frozen surface |
| Kosher salt (Diamond Crystal) | 3g | ~1.5g per fillet (¾ tsp Diamond Crystal total) |
| Black pepper, freshly ground | 1g | |
| Garlic powder | 1g | |
| Smoked paprika | 1g | Color + smoke note |
| Onion powder | 0.5g | Background sweetness |

## Method

1. **Preheat** the Wonder Oven Pro to 400°F (204°C) on **Roast mode**. Roast engages the convection fan to circulate heat evenly around the fillets — critical for thawing the frozen core while pushing the surface past the 300°F Maillard threshold for browning. Place the wire rack in **Position 2** (middle tier).

2. **Prep the fillets directly from frozen.** Run them under cold water for 5 seconds to remove any ice glaze, then pat the surface with paper towels. Brush or rub all surfaces with the 10g olive oil — oil on a frozen surface conducts heat faster than dry air and gives the spices something to grip.

3. **Season immediately.** Mix the 3g salt, 1g pepper, 1g garlic powder, 1g smoked paprika, and 0.5g onion powder. Sprinkle evenly over all oiled surfaces. The oil layer locks spices onto the icy surface where they'd otherwise slide off.

4. **Place fillets skin-side down** on the wire rack set over the drip tray. Space fillets at least 5cm apart so convection air flows freely around each piece.

5. **Roast at 400°F for 18–22 minutes.** The frozen interior thaws gradually while the exterior cooks — this actually produces a more even doneness gradient than starting from thawed, because the outside doesn't overcook waiting for the center to catch up (Lopez-Alt). Start checking at 18 minutes with an instant-read thermometer.

6. **Pull at 120–122°F (49–50°C) internal.** Insert thermometer into the thickest part of the fillet. Carryover will push final temp to **125–130°F (52–54°C)** — the collagen-to-gelatin sweet spot where salmon stays moist and flakes cleanly. Expect +5–8°F rise off heat.

7. **Rest 3 minutes on the rack.** Do not tent with foil — you want the surface to stay dry, not steam. Finish with a squeeze of lemon if you have one.

## Quick Rationale

Cooking salmon from frozen exploits the temperature gradient: the exterior browns while the frozen core gently thaws, yielding more even doneness than starting thawed (Lopez-Alt). Oil on the frozen surface serves as heat conductor, spice adhesive, and Maillard accelerator — overcoming the moisture barrier that ice creates against browning. The Wonder Oven Pro's Roast mode runs convection at 400°F, pushing surface temps past 300°F while circulating air prevents the steam envelope that would stall crisping.

## Notes & Adjustments

- If fillets are thicker than 2.5cm (1 inch), add 3–4 minutes to cook time
- For crispier skin: flip skin-side up for the final 3 minutes and switch to **Broil** mode (rack Position 3)
- Skinless fillets work identically — skip the broil-finish tip
- These times assume individually frozen fillets, not a block of two stuck together

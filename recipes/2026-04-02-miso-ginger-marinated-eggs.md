# Miso-Ginger Marinated Eggs

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Serves:** 4 eggs
**Prep time:** 10 min | **Cook time:** 7 min | **Marinate:** 8–24 hrs | **Total:** ~24 hrs
**Status:** Final

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Large eggs | 4 | Straight from fridge |
| White (shiro) miso | 80g | Mild, sweet — red miso works but saltier |
| Mirin | 40ml (44g) | |
| Sugar | 10g | |
| Fresh ginger | 10g | Finely grated, juice and all |
| Rice vinegar | 5ml (5g) | Optional brightness |

## Method

1. **Boil eggs 6 min 30 sec.** Ice bath 5 min. Peel.
2. **Make miso paste.** Mix miso, mirin, sugar, grated ginger, and rice vinegar into a smooth paste. The miso acts as both marinade and curing agent — its enzymes and salt penetrate the white through osmosis while depositing glutamate (umami) directly.
3. **Wrap each egg.** Coat each egg in a thick layer of paste, then wrap individually in plastic wrap, pressing the paste tight against the surface. Place in a container.
4. **Marinate 8–24 hrs** in the fridge. The miso's proteolytic enzymes gently break down the outer egg white, creating a silky, almost cream-cheese-like texture on the surface.
5. **Serve.** Unwrap, gently wipe off excess paste (don't rinse — leave a thin film). Halve. The yolk will be deeply savory with ginger warmth.

## Quick Rationale

Miso-zuke (miso pickling) is an ancient Japanese preservation technique. The paste's high salt and enzyme content simultaneously seasons and texturally transforms the egg white. Ginger's zingerone compound cuts through the miso's richness.

## Notes & Adjustments

- At 24+ hrs the white becomes very firm and intensely salty — pull at 12 hrs for balance
- Save the used miso paste — stir it into soup or use it to glaze vegetables

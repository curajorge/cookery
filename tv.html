Fresh Pasta Carbonara • Reverse-Sear NY Strip • Arugula Salad — 2026-02-14

Phase 1: First Moves

T − 1:45

Salt the Steak Do First

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Dry-aged NY strip620gUnwrap, bring to counter
Diamond Crystal kosher salt6gWeigh out (~1.5 tsp)
Wire rack + sheet tray1Set up on counter
Paper towels3–4

Instructions

  1. Pat steak bone dry on all sides — surface moisture blocks searing later
  2. Season evenly with 6g kosher salt on all sides
  3. Place on wire rack over tray, leave uncovered
  4. Let sit 45 min minimum — salt draws moisture out, then it reabsorbs carrying salt deep into the meat
Never salt 5–30 min before cooking — draws moisture out without time to reabsorb = wet surface = no sear

Make Pasta Dough Do First

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
00 flour (Caputo)200gWeigh into mixer bowl
Fine salt2gAdd to flour
Whole eggs2 (~100g)Room temp, cracked into a cup
Egg yolk1 (~17g)Room temp, add to cup
Plastic wrap1 sheet

Instructions

  1. Flour + salt in mixer bowl with paddle attachment
  2. Add eggs + yolk, mix on low until shaggy — 1–2 min
  3. Switch to dough hook, knead medium-low 8–10 min until smooth, elastic, springs back when poked
  4. Wrap tight in plastic
  5. Rest 30 min minimum at room temp — gluten must relax or it fights you when rolling
Timer starts now: 30 min rest. Use this time for all mise en place below.

Phase 2: Mise en Place

While Dough Rests • ~25 min

Carbonara Prep

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Guanciale100gCut into 1/2 cm strips (lardons)
Pecorino Romano DOP60gFinely grate on Microplane into a bowl
Egg yolks4 (~68g)Separate — add directly to Pecorino bowl
Whole egg1 (~50g)Add to same Pecorino bowl
Black peppercorns5gCrack coarsely — mortar and pestle, or towel + heavy pan

Combine the Sauce

  1. In one bowl: yolks + whole egg + grated Pecorino + cracked pepper
  2. Whisk until combined — this is your entire sauce
  3. Set aside at room temp (cold sauce = scrambled eggs later)
No cream. Ever. The sauce is egg yolk + cheese + pasta water, emulsified by heat control.

Salad Prep

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Baby arugula80gWash + dry completely (spinner or towels)
Pine nuts20gToast in dry pan 2–3 min, set aside
Parmigiano-Reggiano30gPeeler shavings, not grated — set aside
Fresh lemon juice15gSqueeze half a lemon
Dijon mustard5g~1 tsp
Extra virgin olive oil25gGood quality, fruity
Diamond Crystal kosher salt1gFor the vinaigrette
Flaky sea salt (Maldon)1gFinishing salt — after tossing
Black pepper0.5gFresh cracked

Make the Vinaigrette Now

  1. Small bowl: lemon juice + Dijon + 1g kosher salt
  2. Whisk until salt dissolves — salt in acid first, before oil
  3. Stream in 25g olive oil while whisking — emulsion forms
  4. Taste, adjust salt if needed
  5. Set aside — do NOT dress greens yet (they'll wilt)
Arugula + Parm shavings + pine nuts stay separate until plating. Vinaigrette goes on last minute.

Steak Sear Prep

Set all this by the stove — you'll need it fast later

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Garlic2 cloves (~10g)Smash with knife, leave skin on
Fresh thyme3–4 sprigs (~3g)Leave whole
Unsalted butter30gUnwrap, set on a small plate
Neutral oil10gAvocado or grapeseed — by the cast iron
Black pepper2gCracked — goes on after searing (pepper burns)

Equipment Check

  1. Cast iron skillet — clean, dry, on the stove (not heated yet)
  2. Probe thermometer — for the oven phase
  3. Cutting board + sharp knife — for resting & slicing
  4. Tongs — for the sear

Phase 3: Roll & Cut Pasta

After 30 min Dough Rest • ~15 min

Tonnarelli

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Rested pasta dough~320gUnwrap from plastic
Extra flour or semolina30gFor dusting surface + cut pasta
Sheet tray1For nesting finished pasta

Instructions

  1. Flour the surface generously — dough should not stick at any point
  2. Roll to ~2mm thick — rotate the sheet 90° frequently, don't stretch it
  3. Lightly flour the top of the sheet
  4. Fold loosely into thirds (like a letter)
  5. Cut 2–3mm wide strips for tonnarelli (square-cut spaghetti)
  6. Unfurl the strips, toss in 30g semolina to prevent sticking
  7. Nest loosely on the tray — do not stack or compress
Pasta is ready. Set tray aside — it holds fine at room temp for 1+ hours.

Phase 4: Steak in the Oven

T − 1:00 • Hands-Off 45–55 min

Reverse Sear — Low Oven Phase

Instructions

  1. Set Our Place Oven Pro → Bake → 200°F
  2. Place steak on the wire rack over the tray — elevated for air circulation on all sides
  3. Insert probe thermometer through the thickest part, cable out the door
  4. No flipping needed — the countertop oven surrounds with even heat
  5. Target internal temp: 120°F — estimated 45–55 min
  6. Start checking at 35 min
Everything is prepped. Relax, set the table, open the wine. Just watch the probe.

Phase 5: Go Time

When Probe Hits 120°F • ~25 min to Plating

A. Start Water & Guanciale

Begin this when probe reads ~110°F (~10 min before pull)

Pasta Water

  1. Bring 3L water (3000g) to a rolling boil
  2. Add 30g kosher salt — 1% by weight, non-negotiable
  3. Keep at boil — you'll drop pasta in ~15 min

Render Guanciale

  1. Place 100g guanciale strips in a cold pan
  2. Turn to medium-low heat
  3. Render slowly 8–10 min — fat goes glassy, meat goes crispy
  4. When crispy: add cracked pepper into the fat — 30 sec to bloom the piperine
  5. Kill heat, leave pan on burner — the fat stays warm
Cold start is critical — dropping guanciale into hot oil fries the outside before the fat renders out

B. Pull & Sear the Steak

When probe hits 120°F

Rest (5 min)

  1. Pull steak from Oven Pro
  2. Rest uncovered on a rack — 5 min
  3. Surface dries further = better Maillard. Carryover is minimal (~2–3°F) because oven temp was so low

Sear (3–4 min total)

  1. Heat cast iron on high for 2–3 min — screaming hot
  2. Add 10g neutral oil — should shimmer instantly, just below smoke point
  3. Sear steak 45–60 sec per side — don't move it, let the crust form
  4. Add 30g butter + garlic + thyme, tilt pan
  5. Baste continuously 30–45 sec — spoon foaming butter over the top
  6. Transfer to cutting board

Final Rest (5 min)

  1. Season with 2g cracked black pepper now (pepper burns at sear temps, so it goes on after)
  2. Tent loosely with foil — 5 min
  3. Final internal: 130–135°F (medium) — sear added 10–15°F
Start pasta the moment the steak goes to rest — timing syncs perfectly

C. Drop Pasta & Finish Carbonara

Steak is resting — you have exactly 5 min. Go.

Cook the Pasta (2–3 min)

  1. Drop fresh tonnarelli into the salted boiling water
  2. Cook 2–3 min only — it's fresh, not dried. Taste at 2 min
  3. Before draining: ladle out 150g pasta water into a measuring cup — you need this
  4. Drain pasta (or use tongs to transfer directly to guanciale pan)

Finish the Carbonara (1–2 min)

  1. Transfer pasta into the guanciale pan
  2. TURN OFF HEAT — pan must be OFF. Residual heat only.
  3. Toss pasta in the rendered fat — 15 sec
  4. Pour in the egg/Pecorino/pepper mixture
  5. Add 60–80g reserved pasta water
  6. Toss vigorously for 30–60 sec — the sauce emulsifies into a silky, creamy coating
  7. If too thick: add more pasta water, 15g at a time
  8. If too loose: keep tossing, it thickens as it cools slightly
Residual heat cooks egg to 65–70°C (silky). If pan is too hot → scrambled eggs. If you see curds, add cold pasta water immediately and toss.

D. Dress the Salad

Last minute — greens wilt fast once dressed

Assembly (1 min)

  1. Place 80g arugula in a bowl
  2. Pour vinaigrette over, toss gently with hands or tongs
  3. Add 0.5g cracked black pepper
  4. Top with Parm shavings (30g)
  5. Scatter toasted pine nuts (20g)
  6. Finish with 1g flaky Maldon salt

E. Plate & Serve

Plating

  1. Slice steak against the grain, 1 cm thick — fan on the plate
  2. Pour any board juices over the slices
  3. Twirl carbonara with tongs + a fork into a nest on the plate (or separate bowl)
  4. Salad alongside — keep it tall, don't flatten
Done. Serve immediately — carbonara waits for no one.

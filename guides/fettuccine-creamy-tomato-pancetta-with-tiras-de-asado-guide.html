Fettuccine in Creamy Tomato Pancetta + Tiras de Asado

Fresh pasta & Argentine short ribs — Dinner for two — 2026-03-28

Phase 1: Dough & Salt the Ribs

10 min active + 30 min rest

Fresh Fettuccine Dough Do First

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
00 flour (Tipo 00)200gCaputo red bag
Whole eggs2 (~100g)Room temp
Egg yolk1 (~17g)Room temp
Fine salt2gInto the dough

Instructions

  1. Flour + salt in KitchenAid bowl with paddle. Add eggs + yolk, mix on low until shaggy (1–2 min)
  2. Switch to dough hook — knead on medium-low for 8–10 min. Done when smooth, elastic, springs back when poked
  3. Wrap tight in plastic — rest 30 min at room temp
Gluten must relax before rolling or the dough fights you. Don’t skip the rest — the extra yolk makes this dough richer and more golden than a standard 2-egg pasta (Vetri ratio: 100g flour per egg + enrichment yolk).

Salt the Ribs Do Now

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Flanken-cut short ribs450g~1cm thick, bone-in
Coarse kosher salt5gBoth sides

Instructions

  1. Salt ribs generously on both sides — 5g coarse kosher salt
  2. Rest on a wire rack uncovered for 20 min minimum
Nosrat principle: salt draws surface moisture out, dissolves, then reabsorbs back into the meat — seasoning deeper than surface level. 20 min is the sweet spot for thin flanken cuts. Pat dry before cooking for better Maillard crust.
Both tasks start together. While the dough rests and ribs salt, move to Phase 2 mise en place.

Phase 2: Mise en Place & Roll Pasta

15 min — during dough rest

Prep All Components While Dough Rests

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Garlic10g (2 cloves)Minced
San Marzano tomatoes250gJust under half the can, crush by hand
Heavy cream90gMeasure out
Pecorino Romano40gMicroplane — grate right before use
Parmigiano-Reggiano20gMicroplane
Red chili flakes1g
Black pepper2gFresh cracked
Mise en place is not optional — once heat is on, you’re running three things at once. Everything weighed, prepped, within arm’s reach.

Roll & Cut Fettuccine After 30 Min Rest

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Semolina or extra flour30gFor dusting

Instructions

  1. Divide dough in 4 pieces — keep unused pieces wrapped
  2. KitchenAid roller attachment — start at widest setting, pass through twice per setting, work down to setting 5 (~2mm)
  3. Switch to fettuccine cutter — feed each sheet through
  4. Toss cut pasta in semolina — nest loosely on a tray
Rotate and don’t stretch the sheets. If the dough springs back aggressively, let it rest 5 more minutes. Two passes per roller setting builds even gluten structure (Funke technique).
Pasta is ready. Set tray aside. Move to Phase 3.

Phase 3: Air Fry the Ribs

20 min total — 5 preheat + 14 cook

Tiras de Asado — Wonder Oven Pro Air Fry

Instructions

  1. Preheat Wonder Oven Pro — Air Fry 400°F, 5 min preheat, Deep bake pan on bottom rack to catch drip
  2. Pat ribs completely dry with paper towels — surface moisture is the enemy of Maillard browning
  3. Place ribs on middle rack — single layer, no overlap, bone side down first
  4. Cook 6–7 min per side — flip once. Target: deep brown crust, slightly pink center
  5. Rest on cutting board 3 min — cut between bones to separate into individual pieces
Argentine asado philosophy: salt + fire + good beef. The convection fan in Air Fry mode circulates 400°F air around the ribs — Maillard crust from all sides while intramuscular fat renders through the cross-cut marbling. Less smoke than broil because fat drips down, not up into the element.
Don’t open the oven during cooking — you lose convection heat. One flip at the halfway mark, that’s it.
Ribs resting. Start the sauce and pasta water NOW — you want everything to finish together.

Phase 4: Sauce & Pasta

15 min — everything converges

Render Pancetta & Build Sauce

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Pancetta (cubed)100gAlready in lardons
Garlic (minced)10gFrom Phase 2 prep
Red chili flakes1g
San Marzano tomatoes200gHand-crushed
Heavy cream60g
Black pepper2gFresh cracked

Instructions

  1. Pancetta in cold pan — medium-low, 8–10 min until fat is glassy, edges crisp
  2. Add garlic + chili flakes to the rendered fat — 30 seconds until fragrant
  3. Add hand-crushed tomatoes — simmer 8–10 min, stir occasionally, until sauce thickens and oil separates at edges
  4. Add cream — stir through, simmer 2 min
  5. Season with pepper — hold on salt until after cheese is added
Cold start for pancetta = even render. Fat melts before the exterior crisps, so you get glassy translucent cubes, not burnt shells with raw centers. Bloom chili + garlic in pork fat to activate volatile compounds (McGee: fat-soluble aromatics need a lipid carrier).
Cream stabilizes the tomato emulsion and rounds the acidity — this is the vodka-sauce architecture without the vodka. The rendered pork fat does the same flavor-extraction work.

Cook & Finish Fettuccine Timing Critical

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Water3000g (3L)Boiling
Kosher salt (pasta water)30g1% by weight
Fresh fettuccineAll of itFrom tray
Pecorino Romano40gMicroplaned
Parmigiano-Reggiano20gMicroplaned

Instructions

  1. Boil 3L water + 30g salt — start when pancetta goes in so it’s ready in time
  2. Drop fettuccine — 2–3 min only, it’s fresh
  3. Reserve 100g pasta water before draining
  4. Using tongs, transfer drained fettuccine directly into the pan with the tomato-cream sauce. Add 50g reserved pasta water. Toss over low heat for 30 sec so the pasta absorbs the sauce
  5. Kill the heat. Add pecorino + parm, toss vigorously 15 sec. Too thick? Add more pasta water a splash at a time until it coats the noodles like silk
Fresh pasta cooks in 2–3 min — not 8–10 like dried. Watch it. Overcooked fresh fettuccine turns to mush. Taste at 2 min.
Starch from pasta water + melted cheese + rendered pork fat = emulsified silk. The starch acts as a binder (Lopez-Alt: pasta water concentration is why less water = starchier = better sauce).

Phase 5: Plate & Serve

2 min

The Plate

Instructions

  1. Twirl fettuccine with tongs into the center of each warm plate — let the sauce pool naturally
  2. Lay 2–3 rib pieces alongside the pasta, cut side showing the bone cross-section
  3. Drizzle good EVOO (~10g) over the pasta — adds brightness against the cream and pork fat
  4. Finish: extra grated pecorino over the pasta, cracked black pepper, a pinch of chili flakes if you like heat
Dinner is served. The creamy tomato pasta is rich and silky, the ribs are beefy and simple. Pour something red. Enjoy it with your wife.

One-Pan Chicken, Broccoli & Plantains

Sheet pan dinner — smart probe drives timing — 2026-04-13

Phase 1: Mise en Place

10 min — before oven

Chicken Breast Protein

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Marinated chicken breastas portionedPat surface dry with paper towels — leave marinade flavor, remove surface moisture
Dry surfaces brown. Maillard reaction needs surface temps above 300°F. Moisture = steam = no browning. Even marinated chicken benefits from a quick pat-dry (Lopez-Alt).

Broccoli & Plantains Veg & Starch

Mise en Place

ItemAmountPrep
Broccoli1 head (~300g)Cut into florets, halve any larger than a golf ball
Olive oil (for broccoli)15gToss with broccoli
Kosher salt (Diamond Crystal)3gFor broccoli
Black pepper1gFor broccoli
Garlic powder2gFor broccoli
Frozen plantains1 packageKeep frozen until pan goes in — do not thaw

Instructions

  1. Toss broccoli in a bowl with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Set aside — it goes in last.
  2. Leave plantains in the bag until the moment you add them to the hot pan.
Frozen plantains go in straight from the freezer. The ice flash-steams on the hot pan, then evaporates — after that the surface dries and caramelization begins. No thawing needed.

Oven Setup

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F / 218°C, rack in center position.
  2. Line a sheet pan with foil or parchment for easy cleanup.
  3. Set your smart probe target to 157°F / 69°C.
Oven hot? Probe ready? Pan lined? Move to Phase 2.

Phase 2: Chicken & Plantains

First in the oven — T = 0:00

Load the Pan Probe goes in now

Instructions

  1. Place chicken breast in the center of the sheet pan. Insert the smart probe into the thickest part of the breast, approaching from the side so the tip sits dead-center of the meat.
  2. Arrange frozen plantains in a single layer around the chicken. Give each piece space — crowding traps steam and prevents browning.
  3. Slide pan into oven. Start the probe cook program. The probe now controls your timeline.
Probe placement matters. If the tip touches the pan or pokes through the top, you'll get a false reading. Aim for the geometric center of the thickest section — that's the last point to reach target temp (McGee).
The probe will give you an estimated total cook time. Once you have that number, you know when to add the broccoli: subtract 12 minutes from the estimated finish — that's your broccoli add time.

Phase 3: Add Broccoli

~12 min before probe target — or when probe reads ~120°F

Broccoli Goes In Watch the probe

Trigger

SignalValueAction
Probe reads~120°F / 49°CAdd broccoli now
OR estimated time remaining~12 minAdd broccoli now

Instructions

  1. Pull pan out briefly. Work fast to keep oven heat loss minimal.
  2. Spread seasoned broccoli in a single layer, cut-side down on the pan for maximum char surface.
  3. Flip plantains while the pan is out — they should be starting to color on the bottom side by now.
  4. Return pan to oven. Continue cooking until the probe hits target.
Cut-side down = flat surface on hot metal = direct conduction = Maillard browning on the broccoli. Floret-side down just steams in its own moisture (Lopez-Alt).

Phase 4: Pull & Rest

Probe hits 157°F — 5 min rest

Chicken Rest required

Instructions

  1. Remove chicken from the pan to a cutting board when probe reads 157°F / 69°C.
  2. Rest for 5 minutes — loosely tented with foil. Carryover will push internal temp to 165°F / 74°C.
  3. Slice against the grain after resting.
Do not skip the rest. Cutting immediately loses juice — myosin proteins need time off-heat to gel and trap moisture. 5 minutes minimum (Nosrat, Lopez-Alt).

Broccoli & Plantains Optional extra time

Instructions

  1. Check plantains — they should be golden-brown and caramelized at edges. If not, return the pan (without the chicken) to the oven for 2–3 more minutes.
  2. Check broccoli — tips should be charred, stems tender-crisp. Same fix: a couple extra minutes if needed.
  3. Plate everything once the chicken has rested.
Chicken rested, plantains golden, broccoli charred at the tips? You're done. Plate and eat.
